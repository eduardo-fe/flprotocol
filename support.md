**This system makes you data-driven:**
- Track 3 key metrics
- Read them weekly

# WHAT YOU TRACK

## 1. DAILY WEIGHT (Raw Data)
**When:** Every morning, same time
- After bathroom
- Before eating/drinking
- Naked or same clothes
- Same scale, same spot

**Record in notes app:**
```
Mon: 85.2kg
Tue: 84.8kg
Wed: 85.4kg
Thu: 84.6kg
Fri: 84.9kg
Sat: 85.1kg
Sun: 84.5kg
```

**Don't react to daily numbers.** They fluctuate ±1-2kg from:
- Water retention
- Sodium intake
- Carb timing
- Bowel movements
- Sleep quality

---

## 2. WEEKLY AVERAGE WEIGHT (The Real Signal)
**When:** Calculate every Sunday

**How:**
Add all 7 daily weights → Divide by 7

**Example:**
```
Week 1: (85.2 + 84.8 + 85.4 + 84.6 + 84.9 + 85.1 + 84.5) ÷ 7 = 84.9kg
Week 2: (84.7 + 84.3 + 84.5 + 84.1 + 84.4 + 84.6 + 84.0) ÷ 7 = 84.4kg
Week 3: (84.2 + 83.8 + 84.1 + 83.7 + 84.0 + 84.2 + 83.6) ÷ 7 = 83.9kg
```

**This is your true progress metric.**

---

## 3. WAIST MEASUREMENT (Visual Progress)
**When:** Every Sunday morning
- Fasted state
- After bathroom
- Before eating

**How:**
- Tape measure around navel (not the narrowest point)
- Tape parallel to floor
- Relaxed (don't suck in)
- Same spot every week (use a freckle/mark as reference)

**Record:**
```
Week 0: 94cm (baseline)
Week 1: 93cm
Week 2: 92cm
Week 3: 91cm
```

---

## 4. PERFORMANCE MARKERS (Training Quality)
**Track these in your gym log:**

**Gym Days:**
- Main lift weight (bench/squat/deadlift - whatever you track)
- Reps on last set
- Overall energy (1-10 scale)

**Cardio Days:**
- Run/cycle pace
- Distance covered
- How it felt (easy/moderate/hard)

**Why this matters:**
If performance drops significantly:
→ Deficit too aggressive or carbs too low
→ Time to adjust

# WEEK-BY-WEEK ADJUSTMENT RULES

## WEEK 1-2: EXECUTION PHASE
**Your job:** Follow the plan exactly, collect data

**Expected:**
- Weight: -1 to 2.5kg (mostly water/glycogen)
- Waist: -1 to 2cm
- Energy: Slight dip midweek (normal adaptation)

**Actions:**
- ✅ Meal prep Sunday
- ✅ Weigh all fats/proteins/carbs strictly
- ✅ Track daily weight
- ✅ Measure waist Sunday
- ❌ Don't adjust anything yet

**Red flags (rare, but watch for):**
- Energy crashes hard (can't finish workouts)
- Extreme hunger (constantly thinking about food)
→ If this happens, add 20g carbs post-workout

**Normal sensations:**
- Slight hunger between meals (expected)
- Less "full" after meals (you're eating less)
- Thinking about food more (brain adjusting)

---

## WEEK 3: ASSESSMENT & DECISION POINT

**This is where you decide if the plan is working.**

Calculate your 2-week average change:
```
Week 1 average: 84.9kg
Week 3 average: 83.9kg
Total change: -1.0kg over 2 weeks = 0.5kg/week
```

### SCENARIO A: Losing 0.5-1.0kg/week
**Waist:** Probably down 2-3cm total

**Decision:** ✅ Continue exactly as is
- Plan is working perfectly
- Don't change anything
- Keep executing

**Why:** This is ideal fat loss rate while maintaining muscle/performance

---

### SCENARIO B: Losing <0.3kg/week
**Waist:** Minimal change (maybe 1cm)

**Decision:** ⚠️ Deficit not aggressive enough

**Adjustments (pick ONE):**

**OPTION 1 - Reduce carbs on cardio/rest days**
- Remove 1 slice toast from breakfast
- OR reduce rice portions by 30g (cooked) at lunch/dinner
- Maintains gym day fuel

**OPTION 2 - Tighten fat even more**
- Remove optional evening snacks completely
- Only use fat for cooking, no dressings

**DON'T:**
- Add more cardio yet (creates stress, increases hunger)
- Drop protein (you'll lose muscle)
- Crash diet (will backfire)

**Re-assess in 2 more weeks.**

---

### SCENARIO C: Losing >1.5kg/week
**Performance:** Probably dropping (lifts down, runs feeling hard)

**Decision:** ⚠️ Deficit too aggressive

**Adjustments:**
- Add 30g carbs post-workout (gym days)
- OR add small carb serving to one cardio day meal

**Why:** You're losing too fast, risking muscle loss and metabolic adaptation

**Goal:** Slow to 0.5-1kg/week

---

### SCENARIO D: Not losing, but waist shrinking
**Weight:** Stable or barely changing
**Waist:** Down 2-3cm

**Decision:** ✅ You're recomping
- Losing fat
- Gaining/maintaining muscle
- Scale doesn't show it

**Action:** Continue. Trust the waist measurement.

**Why this happens:**
- You're new-ish to consistent gym training
- Body is building muscle while losing fat
- Especially common in first 4-6 weeks

---

## WEEK 4-6: CONSISTENCY PHASE

**Your job:** Execute, don't overthink

**Expected (if Week 3 adjustments made):**
- Weight: Steady 0.5-0.8kg/week loss
- Waist: Additional 1-2cm
- Performance: Stable or slightly improved

**Actions:**
- Continue meal prep routine
- Keep tracking (can eyeball portions now if you've been precise)
- Monitor performance

**Common challenges:**
- Social events (stick to protein + veg, skip alcohol mostly)
- Boredom (expected — that's the point)
- Plateaus lasting 1 week (normal, water retention masks fat loss)

**When to adjust:**
- If weight stalls 2+ weeks in a row → reduce carbs by 20g on rest day
- If performance tanking → add 20g carbs post-workout
- If constantly starving → add 100 cal from protein/carbs

---

## WEEK 7-8: FINAL PUSH

**Expected total progress by now:**
- Weight: 82-83kg (2-3kg down from Week 3)
- Waist: 88-90cm (4-5cm down from baseline)
- Body composition: Noticeably leaner

**Actions:**
- Keep executing
- Take progress photos (front/side in same lighting as baseline)
- Assess if you want to continue 2-4 more weeks

**Decision point:**
- Happy with waist? → Reverse diet (add 100-150 cal/week back)
- Want to go leaner? → Continue another 4 weeks
- Performance suffering? → Take 1 week at maintenance, then resume

# ADJUSTMENT CHEAT SHEET

## If weight loss stalled 2+ weeks:
1. Reduce carbs by 30g on cardio/rest days (cut toast or rice)
2. Eliminate optional evening snacks
3. Add 1 extra cardio session (light, 30 min)

## If performance dropping:
1. Add 30g carbs post-workout
2. Move more carbs to gym days (steal from rest day)
3. Take a "refeed" day (eat at maintenance, high carb)

## If constantly hungry:
1. Add 100 cal from lean protein
2. Increase vegetable volume (unlimited greens)
3. Drink more water/tea between meals
4. Move calories to earlier in day

## If weekend eating is ruining progress:
1. Prep meals Friday night for Saturday/Sunday
2. Eat same plan on weekends (no "relaxing")
3. Social events: protein + veg, 1-2 drinks max
4. Track Sunday same as weekdays

# PERFORMANCE BENCHMARKS

## What "normal" looks like in a deficit:

### Week 1-2
- Strength: Maintained or very slight dip
- Cardio pace: Maintained
- Energy: 7-8/10 (slight adaptation)

### Week 3-4
- Strength: Should stabilize (might even improve as you get leaner)
- Cardio pace: Maintained or slightly better (lighter bodyweight)
- Energy: 7-9/10

### Week 5-8
- Strength: Stable (maybe 5-10% dip on main lifts is acceptable)
- Cardio pace: Often improves (better power-to-weight)
- Energy: 7-8/10

### RED FLAGS (adjust immediately):
- Strength drops >15% on main lifts
- Cardio pace drops significantly
- Energy consistently <5/10
- Can't complete normal workout volume

# THE PSYCHOLOGY OF TRACKING

## Why daily weight fluctuates (so you don't panic):

**Monday morning:** 84.5kg ✅
**Tuesday morning:** 85.2kg ⚠️ "WTF I gained 0.7kg overnight?"

**Likely reasons:**
- Ate more sodium Monday
- Higher carb intake Monday (holds water)
- Didn't poop yet Tuesday
- Trained hard Monday (inflammation/water retention)
- Slept poorly (cortisol holds water)

**This is NOT fat gain.** It's water/food mass.

**What matters:** Weekly average trend

---

## When you'll see scale movement:

**Week 1:** Big drop (exciting, mostly water)
**Week 2:** Smaller drop or plateau (body adjusting)
**Week 3:** Consistent drop resumes (real fat loss)
**Week 4-8:** Steady 0.5-0.8kg/week (sustainable)

**Visual changes lag scale by 1-2 weeks.**

You'll notice:
- Week 3: Face looks leaner
- Week 4: Waist noticeably smaller
- Week 5: Shirts fit differently
- Week 6-8: Others start commenting

# TROUBLESHOOTING GUIDE

## "I followed it perfectly but nothing changed in 2 weeks"

**Check:**
1. Are you weighing fats/oils? (This is the #1 hidden error)
2. Are you counting weekend meals the same?
3. Are you eating the "optional" evening snack every night? (Cut it)
4. Is there a hidden snack? (Coffee with milk, handful of nuts while cooking)
5. Are you measuring cooked or raw weights consistently?

**90% of "it's not working" = measurement error**

---

## "I'm losing weight but waist not changing"

**Possible causes:**
1. Not measuring waist consistently (same spot, same time)
2. Losing fat from other areas first (normal)
3. Bloating/digestion issues masking waist change
4. Time needed (waist is often the last to shrink)

**Action:** Give it 2 more weeks. If still no waist change but weight dropping → check measurements.

---

## "I'm starving all the time"

**Solutions:**
1. Drink 500ml water before feeling hungry
2. Increase vegetable volume (add 200g more greens to meals)
3. Move more calories to earlier in day
4. Add 10g protein to each meal
5. Check sleep (poor sleep = more hunger hormones)

**If still starving after these:** Add 100 cal from lean protein or carbs

---

## "My lifts are tanking"

**First check:**
- Sleep quality (7+ hours?)
- Recovery between gym days (48 hours?)
- Stress levels (work/life overwhelming?)

**If all good but still weak:**
- Add 30g carbs to post-gym meal
- Consider moving more carbs to gym days from rest days
- Take a deload week (reduce weights 20%, maintain volume)

---

## "I cheated on the weekend"

**Don't spiral.**

**Option 1 - Minor cheat (+300-500 cal one day):**
- Get back on plan Monday
- No compensation (don't skip meals or add cardio)
- It'll cost you 2-3 days of progress, not the whole week

**Option 2 - Major cheat (+1000+ cal over 2 days):**
- You probably erased that week's deficit
- Don't panic or restrict harder
- Resume plan Monday
- Accept it delays results by 1 week
- Learn from it (what triggered it? How to prevent next time?)

**The goal:** Don't let 1 bad weekend become 2, then 3.
